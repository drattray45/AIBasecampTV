# Magic Moment

*Drafted: June 2026. Generated from `docs/PRODUCT.md` + `design/1. Product Identity.md`.*

> **Architecture note:** the three candidates below are not rivals for one slot — they're **layers on one activation event.** The primary is Candidate 2 (its metric *is* the Phase-0 north star). Candidate 1 sits in front of it as a deliberately *thin* pre-signup teaser that makes the email gate convert. Candidate 3 is not a separate option — it's the design spec for the *center* of the primary task. One activation event, a cheap belief-builder in front, a judgment-moment built into its core.

## The recommended magic moment

> When a nervous beginner completes her **first home-turf task in session one** — she asks AI about a topic she already knows, gets an obviously-good answer, and *can tell it's right because it's her own subject* — she feels *"I did that, and I'm not helpless in front of this thing."* That's the moment she stops being someone who closed the tab and becomes someone who's started.

**Where it sits:** First session, inside the free Starter Kit (preceded by a thin pre-signup teaser — see Candidate 1).
**Time-to-aha target:** Task 1 completed in <5 minutes of opening the kit; the pre-signup teaser fires in <60 seconds on the landing page.
**Success metric:** % of Starter Kit signups who complete task 1 in session 1 — **this is the strategy's Phase-0 "activated / qualified subscriber" north star.** Aha, activation, and north star are the same instrumented number, by design.

## Why this is the primary

It's the only candidate whose metric is the number you've already committed to steering the business by — so you don't celebrate one number and steer by another. Candidate 1's metric (landing-visitor completion) is upstream of the email and Candidate 3's (a feeling survey) is soft to instrument by their own admission; only Candidate 2 *is* the north star firing. Its one real weakness — it's gated behind an email she gives before she's felt anything — is solved not by replacing it but by putting a thin Candidate-1 teaser in front of the gate, and its core feeling ("I can tell it's right") is guaranteed by building Candidate 3 into the task itself.

## Three candidates considered

### Candidate 2: "I did my first one" — session-1 home-turf task **(PRIMARY)**

- **Action:** Complete the Starter Kit's first home-turf task in session 1 (then all five across the week).
- **Wow:** "I just did a real AI task on my own subject — and I could tell it was right." By five: "I did five real things with AI this week."
- **Where:** First session (task 1) → day 7 (all five).
- **Time-to-aha:** Task 1 in <5 minutes of opening the kit.
- **Comparable evidence:** Duolingo (first lesson + XP + streak; 55% MoM DAU retention; "progress feeling"); free-mini-course best practice ("one meaningful, outcome-driven win").
- **Risk:** Gated behind email, so she commits before she's reassured — mitigated by the C1 teaser. Metric can drift to "completed all 5" (too late) — keep it pinned on task-1-in-session-1.
- **Metric:** % of Starter Kit signups completing task 1 in session 1 (= Phase-0 north star); deeper, % completing all 5 within 7 days.

### Candidate 1: "It got me" — thin pre-signup teaser **(LAYER IN FRONT)**

- **Action:** Before any email, she sees one pre-filled example — a real topic with a visibly great AI answer she can simply *read* (not even type) — captioned "this is what a good first question gets you; yours is next."
- **Wow:** A single "oh, it got that" beat that manufactures enough belief to make the signup feel safe.
- **Where:** Pre-signup (landing page / first-video companion). **Time-to-aha:** <60 seconds.
- **Comparable evidence:** Lovable (pre-signup value in ~60s), Perplexity (no-login answer), Duolingo (sub-60s first value).
- **Risk:** A *richly scaffolded* interactive box is real build cost and, if under-scaffolded, re-stages the blank-box failure. So **ship it thin** — a read-only worked example clears the bar. Invest in a fuller interactive version only if C2's activation rate shows the gate is where you're losing people.
- **Metric:** Landing → Starter Kit signup conversion (does the teaser lift it).

### Candidate 3: "I can tell it's right" — the design spec for task 1's center **(LAYER INSIDE)**

- **Action:** Inside task 1, she judges the AI's answer against her own expertise — and we explicitly cue her to ("you know this — did it get it right? miss anything?").
- **Wow:** "I'm not helpless in front of this — I can tell when it's good." (Heals Pain #2: she can't tell if AI output is right, so won't trust it.) This judgment *is* the reassurance.
- **Where:** First session, built into task 1 (not a separate moment). **Time-to-aha:** <5 minutes.
- **Comparable evidence:** Perplexity (sources to verify); AIBasecamp's own Pillar-1 "click a source to fact-check" habit.
- **Risk:** Internal feeling, not a clean event — so it's *built in*, not measured as the primary. Its survey is secondary quality instrumentation.
- **Metric:** One-tap post-task micro-survey ("how did that feel?" — not the primary count, a read on *quality* of the win).

## Task 1 — build spec for the activation event

*The magic moment only fires if task 1 is built to produce both beats — "it got me" and "I can tell it's right" — with zero blank-box friction.*

**The task:** "Ask AI about something you already know."

**Scaffolding (she never faces a blank box):**
1. She picks her turf from a short menu — *my job · a hobby I'm good at · something I cook · a place I know well · a topic I could talk about for an hour* — or types one word.
2. We hand her a **pre-written prompt** with her topic slotted in, e.g.: *"I know a lot about **[HER TOPIC]**. Explain it to a smart beginner in about 150 words, and name one mistake people commonly make. Plain language, no jargon."* She sends it as-is — no prompting skill required.

**Beat 1 — "it got me":** because *we* wrote the prompt, the answer is good — the deliberate contrast with the bland answer she got last time. Frame it (Scout voice): *"That bland answer before wasn't you failing — it was the wrong question. Watch what a good one does."*

**Beat 2 — "I can tell it's right" (the judgment cue):** it's her expert topic, so she can evaluate it — and we *make her*: *"You know this. Read it — did it get it right? Did it miss anything?"* The embedded "one common mistake" gives her a concrete claim to check against her own knowledge. Then close: *"You just judged an AI answer using what you already know. That's the whole skill. You weren't behind — you just needed the first step."*

**One-tap micro-survey (secondary instrumentation, Scout voice — note: never "was that easy?"):**
*"How did that feel?"* → **[I did it 🙂]** · **[Sort of — still a bit unsure]** · **[Still stuck]**
("I did it" = clean activated win · "Sort of" = completed-but-shaky, send a reassurance nudge · "Still stuck" = activation failed, trigger help.)

**Instrumentation summary:** primary/north-star = task-1-in-session-1 completion %; secondary = the "how did that feel" distribution (win quality); upstream = landing → signup lift from the thin teaser.
