## 1. Proven Demand

*Where is money already flowing for this problem?*

> Good: 2+ competitors clearing $50K+ MRR on the same core problem
> Bad: "no competitors yet" — that's almost always a sign nobody pays for it

**Your answer:** **Wedge CONFIRMED (June 21, 2026 research) — the demand is proven, and there's a positioning gap inside it.** Three monetizing players validate that non-technical adults pay to learn AI from jargon-free creators:

- **Heather Murray / "AI for Non-Techies" (nontechies.ai) — the direct comp.** Live and on-wedge: explicitly "learn AI without the jargon," for non-technical professionals. Started as a newsletter (May 2023) → training site (Mar 2024) → 12-week on-demand course + membership/academy + a *free* "AI Academy" literacy course as the lead magnet, with a 14-day refund. Module values ~£195/$248. Top-20 MarTech influencer, Forbes features, partnerships (Salesforce, Nokia, Starling). **Note her model: membership + free-academy funnel + a "Become an AI Trainer" B2B upsell — she skews toward marketing pros, teams, and aspiring trainers, not the scared personal-money home buyer.**
- **Section / Section School (Scott Galloway, sectionai.com) — adjacent, well-funded.** "AI for business" curriculum for knowledge workers; ~$600 crash course, AI mini-MBA, 50-course roadmap. Skews business-professional/career.
- **Superhuman AI (Zain Kahn) — proves the funnel shape.** Free AI newsletter to 1M+ readers, seven-figure annual revenue within ~4 months (largely ads), plus career-focused AI courses. The free-content→monetize playbook AIBasecamp intends, proven at scale (younger-professional skew).

**Verdict:** not whitespace (de-risks the thesis) and not saturated for *your exact buyer*. Every named comp leans **B2B / career / marketing-pro**; the **personal, home, reassurance-led "I feel behind" buyer (Diane) is adjacent-served, not owned** — proven demand *plus* a differentiated lane. Also note: Heather Murray's free-academy lead magnet + membership confirm both the funnel shape and that a Phase-2 membership is viable in this space. (Sources in footer.)

## 2. Existing Distribution

*Where will the first 10 users come from?*

> Good: a named subreddit, Discord, Facebook group, TikTok hashtag, or SEO keyword
> Bad: "we'll run ads", "we'll go viral", "word of mouth"

**Your answer:** **Candidate surfaces identified (June 21, 2026) — but live composition verification is still your job before posting.** Surface is **Facebook + LinkedIn, NOT Reddit/TikTok/Discord** (Reddit is where the hype-bros she's hiding from gather). Named candidates to evaluate:

- **Facebook — tool/identity groups, *not* "AI" groups:** QuickBooks/bookkeeping user communities (e.g., *5 Minute Bookkeeping with QuickBooks Online*, *QB Power User Community*) and women's professional networks (*Dreamers & Doers*, *Everywoman* ~30K, *Women in Revenue* ~7K). **Caveat from the research:** the most-findable bookkeeper/women-in-business groups skew toward *practice-owners and entrepreneurs*, not employed mid-career Dianes — so finding her *employed-manager* channel needs care. The tool-user groups (where "anyone tried the new AI feature?" threads surface) are the closer match than the business-building groups.
- **LinkedIn:** *Connect: Professional Women's Network* (~500K members) is a strong candidate surface. **Important channel-mechanics change:** LinkedIn removed *followable* hashtags in late 2024 — hashtags are now categorization signals, not browsable discovery feeds. So the LinkedIn play is **commenting under "AI and jobs / upskilling" posts and posting into the algorithm** (#Upskilling, #Reskilling, #CareerChange as tags on your own posts), *not* browsing a hashtag feed.

**Verification test (apply before trusting any candidate):** *are the posters Diane, or people selling to Diane?* If the threads are mostly coaches and creators, it's a marketer pool — discard it. Search can name candidates; only you, inside the group, can confirm it's stranger-Dianes. `[verify composition live before posting]`

> *Note: the brand's faceless Scout channel is the intended long-term distribution engine, but it does not exist yet. **Do not build the channel to validate** — validating via a buy button + honest posts is cheap; building months of video first is the Over-Polished Pre-Launch trap. Validate the offer, then build the channel on confirmed signal.*

## 3. Validation Test

*What's the cheapest test that manufactures a real signal from a stranger?*

> Good: buy button, pre-sale with calendar commitments, concierge MVP, smoke test
> Bad: waitlist, "would you pay?" survey, asking your friends

**Your answer:** Two named tactics, run in parallel, **kept in separate columns and never averaged** — they answer different questions and are non-substitutable.

- **PRIMARY — #5 Buy Button Test** on the **$17 "5 AI Tasks" pack.** A real Stripe checkout, driven by honest, story-framed, value-first posts in the named channels, **deep-linked to the pack (never the homepage), with a UTM.** Isolates the one scalable question validation exists for: *will Diane swipe with no human in the loop?* (Routed via the decision tree: "audience is a tightly-defined community under 100K" → #10 Niche Sub-Community Smoke Test, executed as a #5 buy-button card-swipe.)
- **PARALLEL — #12 Concierge MVP** at **$99 (founding-framed: lowest price it'll ever be, testimonial a term of entry).** "I'll personally get you doing your first 5 AI tasks this week." This is the **proof-and-data engine** — it produces the before/after **testimonials** (the offer's missing Proof element) and the real-time **beginner drop-off data** that is the only raw material for the Basecamp Path moat. Start same-day or slightly earlier than #5 so its language and failure-data can sharpen the pack. **It does not validate the scalable offer** — a concierge yes is partly *you* selling, so it proves "the method works," not "the self-serve offer converts." Hold that line.

## 4. Pass Threshold

*What concrete outcome means "yes, keep going"?*

> Good: 1+ unsolicited card swipe, 10+ calendar bookings, 20+ paying users in 30 days
> Bad: upvotes, email signups, "I'd totally use this!" replies

**Your answer:** Each test has its own pass line, window, and the specific thing it proves. A count alone can't pass — every bar carries a **denominator or a definition** so a lucky-but-failing result can't sneak through.

### Validation scorecard

| Test | Pass line | Window | What it proves | Guard against |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **#5 Buy Button** ($17 pack) | **3+ COLD stranger card swipes AND click-to-buy ≥ 2–3% of qualified traffic** | **14 days OR 300+ qualified visitors — whichever is the real test** | The self-serve offer **scales** | The rate is the verdict; the count of 3 just guards against reading noise. "Cold" = no one who knows you, no warm list — or you validate your network, not the market. Tie the window to the **traffic floor**: a quiet 14 days of trickle is not a fair trial. |
| **#12 Concierge** ($99 founding) | **2–3 completions-with-testimonial** (weight toward **2 = a real pass**) | **21 days** | The **method works** + yields proof & Path data | **Count completions, not sales.** A paid no-show gives neither proof nor data — the entire point. Pass = finished transformations with a captured before/after, not card swipes. |

★★★★★ band on both (cold stranger card swipe = the only signal that survives every other test). The two never substitute: #5's currency is swipes, #12's is completions.

## 5. Pivot Criteria

*What result within 2-4 weeks means you pivot your product offer?*

> Good: a specific number tied to time (e.g. "zero card swipes after 3 demo posts in 14 days")
> Bad: vague, open-ended, "we'll see how it feels"

**Your answer:** **Diagnose before pivoting.** Do NOT pivot on <14 days, <6 posts, or a dead/unverified channel — that's a distribution or patience problem, not a pivot (people-who-try-it-love-it-but-can't-get-strangers-in = run more reps; same-friction-bounce = execution fix). Pivot only after **4+ weeks of real distribution** with two-or-more pivot signals true. Change **one variable per cycle**; if two feel needed, **persona first** (the wrong audience makes every other test lie).

- **Persona pivot trigger:** channel confirmed-active, real traffic driven (≥6 posts / ~200+ qualified visits to the pack) over 14–21 days, **Diane engages but ~0 cold swipes, AND a different segment (the freelancer/owner) is the one asking or swiping** → **persona-pivot to the freelancer/owner secondary** (strategy already flagged it may out-convert). Re-write the Offer for the new persona, re-run #5 (#10 smoke test) in SMB/freelancer channels. Keep product + price band. Pass: trial-to-paid ≥ 2× the Diane attempt, 5+ paying from the new persona in 30 days.
- **Price pivot trigger:** healthy traffic, **people click Buy then bounce at the $17 checkout** (clicks, no completes) → **price-pivot** (test a free→email-gate, or $9, or a reframe). Change only price. Pass: click-to-checkout completion ≥ 2× baseline + 3 cold swipes in 14 days.
- **NOT a pivot:** zero engagement on the posts at all → **distribution problem**. Run 10 more posts in different named channels before concluding anything. (Most common false-pivot for a cold founder.)
