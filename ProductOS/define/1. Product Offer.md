## Describe your product using your product offer

**AIBasecamp.TV:** For non-technical adults 40–60 who feel professionally exposed by AI, to help them stop secretly concluding *"I'm just not getting it"* and instead do five real AI tasks in their first week — and get hours back every month after — AIBasecamp does this using the Basecamp Path, the proven order of the five things that matter, guided by Scout, a guide who makes it safe to be a beginner; backed by peer proof it must still build, and de-risked by a 60-day *"if it doesn't click, that's on us, not you"* money-back promise.

## 1. Customer

*Who exactly is this for?*

> Good: specific, searchable, high value
> Bad: vague, broad, fuzzy

**Your answer:** Non-technical adults ~40–60 (written to the 48-year-old at the center of the dread, not excluding 35s) in ordinary white-collar or self-employed work, who have already opened ChatGPT once or twice, felt lost, and quietly closed the tab — and who feel *professionally exposed* by AI: the employee watching younger colleagues pull ahead, the freelancer/owner afraid of being undercut, or the near-retirement worker afraid of being managed out for "not keeping up." Curiosity-seekers are the broad free-content audience; the *exposed* are the paying wedge. `[validate — no launch data; the three exposure flavors pay and search differently, confirm which is biggest/reachable via live search + first 90 days of comments]`

## 2. Pain

*What problem are they struggling with right now?*

> Good: specific, urgent, costly
> Bad: vague, mild, hypothetical

**Your answer:** They tried it themselves — opened ChatGPT, typed a vague question, got a bland generic answer, and concluded *"I'm just not getting it."* It reads as a verdict on their own capability, not on their inputs. And now it's surfacing everywhere — a colleague drops "I just had AI do it" in a meeting and they nod along — so private self-blame compounds into public dread. (Solo failure is the hook: it's universal, low-shame to admit, self-blaming in a way reassurance dissolves, and the exact thing the product repairs. Social moment is the amplifier in hooks/Shorts.) `[collect verbatim language — 5 real quotes from r/ChatGPT & r/artificial "I don't get it" threads, replies on beginner-AI videos, 1–3★ Amazon reviews on beginner-AI books, + text 3 in-bracket people: "what's your honest reaction to all the AI stuff right now?"]`

---

## 3. Outcome

*What concrete result will they get?*

> Good: specific, tangible, measurable
> Bad: vague, broad, fuzzy

**Your answer:** Do 5 real AI tasks in your first week — and start getting hours back every month after that. (Finish line first, ROI trailing: the five-task completion is countable and converts "maybe I can't do this" into "I did five"; the time-savings is the retention/identity promise they only feel on the far side.) **Governing rule: market to the fear, guarantee only the capability** — the hook can name the job ("stop nodding along at work"), but the promise is only ever about what *they* can now do, never their job, boss, income, or anyone else's behavior.

---

## 4. Mechanism

*What process or features deliver the outcome, and why does it work?*

> Good: unique, based on experience/knowledge/data, proven
> Bad: untested, unclear, undefined

**Your answer:** Free content fails this person not because the information is missing but because it's *infinite, unsequenced, and pitched over their head* — which is what made them quit. The load-bearing moat is **the proven order to learn the five things that matter** — an opinionated, version-dated path earned by watching where real beginners actually quit, not a list anyone could publish. Wrapped around it: exact copy-paste prompts that repair the "didn't know what to type" wall, and **Scout** (cartoon-bear guide) who makes it safe to be a beginner — the retention and word-of-mouth engine, not the defensibility. *Today the sequence is a tested hypothesis, not yet an asset; it converts into a moat through the first 90 days of funnel data (comments, Starter-Kit drop-off, which Short triggers "oh, that's what I was doing wrong") — R&D almost no competitor is patient enough to mine.*

**The Basecamp Path, v1 — thesis:** *Confidence before capability.* A scared beginner won't attempt anything real until they've felt one unmistakable win in their own words, so the order is rigged to manufacture that win in task one and compound from there. Beginners quit for two reasons — blank-prompt paralysis, and flashy tools hitting before they trust themselves — so the path removes the blank page first and defers the dazzle to last:

1. **"It gets you"** — ask AI about something you already know (job/hobby); the win lands because they can judge the answer on home turf. *Must be frictionless: notice, don't produce; win visible in seconds.*
2. **"Fix my words"** — rewrite a real awkward email they paste in (no prompting skill required; first useful artifact, near-zero stakes).
3. **"Explain it like I'm smart but new"** — decode something confusing (a form, a clause, a letter); utility turns personal.
4. **"Do the boring thing"** — summarize a long document / prep a meeting; first felt time-savings.
5. **"Make something"** — one creative/visual win to show someone; deliberately *last* so the dazzle is word-of-mouth fuel, not an overwhelm trigger.

*Survival check: foundation-model releases feed this, not threaten it — more hype → more "I'm behind" dread → more buyers. The bottleneck is human fear, which no model release fixes. Counter-cyclical.*

## 5. Guarantee

*What bold promise reduces buyer risk?*

> Good: high value, bold, tied to the outcome
> Bad: low value, low commitment, detached from outcome

**Your answer:** *Try the whole path for 60 days, at whatever pace feels right — there's genuinely no rush, and you're not behind. Do the five tasks, and if you reach the end and still don't feel comfortable using AI for the everyday stuff, just email us and we'll send back every penny. And if something didn't click for you? That's on us to teach better — not on you to try harder. You'll never have to prove you "did the work." Your word is enough.* (You'll never need a paid AI subscription to finish, and we keep the path current as the tools change.) — Defuses the real killer objection ("what if *I'm* the problem") by putting blame on the path, never the buyer; 60 days over "finish anytime" to keep urgency-to-start. `[Operator decision — only Drew can sign this; sign only if payable instantly without resentment, including a few % who take the win and the refund. Measure refund-abuse % from launch.]`

## 6. Proof

*What evidence shows this actually works?*

> Good: testimonials, case studies, reviews, results
> Bad: no results, vague, impersonal

**Your answer:** Founder credibility re-aimed from *status to service* — three years running an AI-focused agency (AFA) plus a product-design background, framed not as "expert teaching down to you" but as *"I've spent three years deep in these tools and made every mistake so you don't have to — I built the path I wish someone had handed a beginner."* That credential's job is **curation credibility** (why the order is trustworthy), never connection credibility; it sits quietly under peer framing, never as the headline, because this buyer connects before they evaluate. **Frame is peers-not-tech:** never flash ARR, Product Hunt, or tech-press logos — they widen the very gap the brand exists to close. `[BINDING GAP — zero student results; practitioner proof ≠ peer/outcome proof. No beginner has crossed the path yet. This is the gating constraint on the paid launch, not a copy problem.]`

**Proof build-order (to manufacture, first 90 days):** (1) one real beginner's before/after in their own words — highest value, get it first; (2) peer YouTube comments (*"I'm 54 and this finally made sense"*) + watch metrics — the channel's job before any paid push; (3) completion counts (*"11,000 first AI tasks done with Scout"*); (4) named peer-mirror testimonials (real name, age, ordinary job); (5) before/after task screenshots.

## Validation appendix — turn the priors into testable claims

The offer is built on reasoned priors because there is no audience yet. Before scaling spend, confirm or kill these:

**Segment search-test (which exposure flavor pays).** Build one piece of content / one hook per flavor and watch which converts:

- *Displaced employee* — likely searches: "how to use AI at work," "ChatGPT for my job," "AI skills for my career." Title that catches: "Stop nodding along when AI comes up at work."
- *Undercut freelancer/owner* — likely searches: "AI for small business," "competitors using AI," "AI to save time in my business." Title: "Your competitors are using AI. Here's the beginner's catch-up."
- *Managed-out near-retirement worker* — likely searches: "too old to learn AI," "AI for over 50," "keep my job AI." Title: "Think you're too late for AI? Start here (made for 50+)."

**Verbatim pain hunt (one evening).** Five places: r/ChatGPT & r/artificial "I don't get it" threads; reply sections on beginner-AI YouTube videos; 1–3★ Amazon reviews on beginner-AI books; in-bracket Facebook groups; and text 3 real people you know: *"What's your honest reaction to all the AI stuff right now?"* Capture words verbatim, tics and all. Bring them back to identify which phrases recur and carry the most charge — those become the headline and the Starter Kit's first line.

**Other launch-one measurements:** refund-abuse %; Starter-Kit drop-off point (re-cuts the Basecamp Path order); which Short triggers "oh, that's what I was doing wrong"; whether 60-day beats 14-day on net.
