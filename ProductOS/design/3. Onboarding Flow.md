# Onboarding Flow

*Drafted: June 2026. Generated from `docs/PRODUCT.md`, `design/1. Product Identity.md`, and `design/2. Magic Moment.md`.*

## Summary

A **Try-Before-Signup funnel** (B2B AI SaaS reference for the gate mechanics; Mobile consumer reference for the emotional register) — value before the email, then a one-tap qualifier, then the activation task. **6 screens, web/browser**, estimated **~5–6 minutes to the magic moment** (the pre-signup belief beat fires in <60s; the full activation task is <5 min). The magic moment is **Screen 4**.

> *Reference guard: we take Try-Before-Signup's pre-gate-value mechanics only — none of its B2B team/workspace/seat scaffolding. Diane is a consumer spending her own money, not a team buyer.*

## The magic moment we're engineering toward

> When a nervous beginner completes her first home-turf task in session one — asks AI about a topic she already knows, gets an obviously-good answer, and can tell it's right *because it's her own subject* — she feels "I did that, and I'm not helpless in front of this thing."

**Position:** Screen 4 of 6.
**Time-to-aha target:** Task 1 completed in <5 minutes (pre-signup teaser beat in <60s).
**Success metric:** % of Starter Kit signups who complete task 1 in session 1 — the strategy's Phase-0 "activated / qualified subscriber" north star.

## The flow

### Screen 1 — Landing + pre-signup teaser ("It got me")

- **Goal:** Manufacture one "oh, it got that" belief beat *before any ask*, using a read-only worked example (the cheap, cold-founder version of the Live Prompt Box — ship thin).
- **User action:** Read the worked example; optionally tap a different topic chip to see another; click the CTA.
- **Copy:**
  - Headline: "See what a good question gets you."
  - Sub: "Here's a real AI answer about growing tomatoes. Yours is next — no tech skills, nothing to install, no sign-up to look."
  - CTA: "Try it with my topic →"
- **Wireframe:** Worked-example card (a pre-filled prompt on "gardening" + a visibly good answer she can *read*); a row of topic chips (gardening · cooking · managing a team · your job); one primary CTA. No input box, no email yet.
- **Drop-off risk:** If it reads as hype, or asks her to type into a blank box, she bounces or re-stages the failure that made her quit. Keep it read-only and concrete.
- **Reference:** B2B AI SaaS Tactic #1 (Live Prompt Box), adapted read-only for a cold founder; Mobile consumer "show the win first."

### Screen 2 — Email gate ("save this + get the other four")

- **Goal:** Capture email at the moment of crystallized interest — framed as *getting more / not losing*, never "sign up."
- **User action:** Tap Google/Apple SSO, or type email; submit.
- **Copy:**
  - Headline: "Want to do that with your own topic?"
  - Sub: "Get the free Starter Kit — your first 5 AI wins, in the right order. No cost, no spam."
  - CTA: "Send me the Starter Kit" (SSO above: "Continue with Google" / "Continue with Apple")
- **Wireframe:** SSO buttons above an email field; one CTA; a small reassurance line ("no card, unsubscribe anytime"). Email only — no other fields.
- **Drop-off risk:** A generic email wall bounces. Mitigated because the teaser already paid out. Never ask for more than the email here.
- **Reference:** B2B AI SaaS Tactic #2 (Signup Gate at "Save") + Tactic #3 (SSO above the email field).

### Screen 3 — "What best describes you?" (the qualified-subscriber beat)

- **Goal:** A one-tap self-ID that *instruments the Phase-0 qualified-subscriber metric* and enables per-persona activation segmentation (how you'd later detect the "freelancer out-converts Diane → persona pivot" signal). A beat, not a wall.
- **User action:** One tap among three options (or skip).
- **Copy:**
  - Headline: "Quick one — what's true for you?"
  - Sub: "So we point you at what helps most. One tap."
  - Options: "I work in an office / manage a team" · "I run my own thing" · "I'm just curious"
  - (Tap advances; subtle "skip" link.)
- **Wireframe:** Three large tappable options; faint skip link. No typing, no second question.
- **Drop-off risk:** Feels like a survey if it's more than one tap. Keep it literally one tap, skippable, no typing.
- **Reference:** B2B AI SaaS Tactic #4 (Ask Exactly One Question, And Make It Routing) — repurposed as the persona-routing qualifier (maps to the three personas: employee/Diane, freelancer, curious).

### Screen 4 — Starter Kit, Task 1: "Ask about something you already know" ★

- **Goal:** Deliver the activation event — the frictionless home-turf win — with both beats firing: "it got me" *and* "I can tell it's right." Pre-filled prompt scaffolding, never a blank box.
- **User action:** Pick her topic from a menu (or one word) → send the pre-written prompt → read the answer → judge it (we cue her) → tap the one-tap "how did that feel?".
- **Copy:**
  - Headline: "Your turn. Pick something you already know."
  - Sub: "We've written the question for you — just choose your topic and watch."
  - Judgment cue (after the answer): "You know this — read it. Did it get it right? Miss anything?"
  - Reframe (after): "That's the whole skill. You weren't behind — you just needed the first step."
  - Micro-survey: "How did that feel?" → [I did it 🙂] · [Sort of — still a bit unsure] · [Still stuck]
  - CTA: "Nice — what's next →"
- **Wireframe:** Topic chips/menu → a *pre-filled* prompt box (read-only template with [topic] slotted in) → a generated-answer card → the judgment-cue line → the 3-option "how did that feel?" → CTA.
- **Drop-off risk:** A blank box re-stages the original failure — it *must* be the topic menu + pre-written template. If the answer isn't obviously good, the beat misfires — pre-test the template prompts so they reliably produce strong, on-topic answers.
- **Reference:** B2B AI SaaS Tactic #5 (pre-populated, no empty state) + #7 (suggested-prompt rail → here, the pre-written prompt) + #8/#9 (show-your-work / persisted artifact → her own-turf judgment). Magic Moment doc, Task 1 build spec.
- **Magic moment:** This is where she hits the activation event — task-1-in-session-1 completed (= the Phase-0 north star firing).

### Screen 5 — "One down, four to go — no rush" (checklist consolidation)

- **Goal:** Consolidate the win into calm momentum — the remaining four tasks as a gentle one-a-day checklist, never an overwhelming wall.
- **User action:** See the 5-task checklist (task 1 ✓); start task 2, or opt into a tomorrow nudge.
- **Copy:**
  - Headline: "One down. Four to go — no rush."
  - Sub: "Do one a day and you're done by Friday. We'll send a gentle nudge so you don't have to remember."
  - Checklist: 1. Ask about what you know ✓ · 2. Fix an awkward email · 3. Make sense of something confusing · 4. Hand off a boring task · 5. Make something to show someone
  - CTA: "Do task 2 now" (secondary: "Email me tomorrow's step")
- **Wireframe:** Checklist with task 1 checked and the rest calm/de-emphasized; primary + secondary CTA.
- **Drop-off risk:** Showing all five as a to-do list can overwhelm — the opposite of "we removed the firehose." Check task 1 done, frame "one a day, no rush," keep it calm.
- **Reference:** B2B AI SaaS Tactic #10 (3–5 item always-visible checklist); tone from Product Identity (calm, anti-FOMO, "no rush").

### Screen 6 — Soft bridge to the $17 pack

- **Goal:** A gentle, post-win, behavior-triggered bridge to the first paid product — framed as a shortcut, never fear or pressure (the graduation belief forbids it).
- **User action:** See the optional offer; get the pack, or keep going free.
- **Copy:**
  - Headline: "Want the shortcut?"
  - Sub: "The $17 pack has all five tasks written out with the exact words to type — so you never face a blank box again. Optional; the free path works too."
  - CTA: "Get the pack — $17" (secondary: "I'm good with the free path")
  - [Reserved: a real student before/after testimonial, once earned — per Visual World "photography is earned-proof only."]
- **Wireframe:** Pack card ($17, what's inside) + primary CTA + secondary "free path" link; placeholder slot for the earned peer testimonial.
- **Drop-off risk:** Pushing the sale hard betrays the "we won't pressure you" brand and the self-binding belief. Keep it optional, value-framed, post-win — no FOMO words.
- **Reference:** B2B AI SaaS Tactic #16 (behavior-triggered upgrade, post-value), softened for the Everyman/anti-pressure brand; ties to Business Strategy's $17 tripwire.

## Anti-patterns avoided

- **The blank-box first prompt** (B2B AI SaaS "Generic First Prompt" + "Empty Dashboard") — Screen 4 ships a topic menu + pre-written prompt; she never faces an empty box, which is the exact thing that made her quit.
- **The 12-field signup form** — Screen 2 asks for email/SSO only; the one qualifier is a separate one-tap beat, not a form.
- **Gating value behind the email** — the teaser (Screen 1) pays out *before* the gate, so signup is "get more," not "prove it to me."
- **The mobile over-ask (20-question quiz)** — exactly one one-tap question, skippable.
- **The hard paywall / FOMO close** — Screen 6 is an optional, post-win, value-framed bridge; no "don't get left behind," consistent with the brand's kill-list and graduation belief.

## Success metric

**Primary:** % of Starter Kit signups who complete task 1 in session 1 (Phase-0 north star — aha = activation = the number you steer by). **Supporting:** landing → signup conversion (does the teaser lift it); self-ID distribution (persona mix, for the pivot signal); "how did that feel?" distribution (win *quality*); task-1 → task-2 continuation (momentum into the week).
